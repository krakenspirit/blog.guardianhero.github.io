Games

The kind of games I am working on prototyping are games that give the user creative freedom, with some strategy and crafting components:

  • SIM - style games.
  • Runescape / Oregon Trail / Dwarf Fortress
  • Ancestors / Ancient Odyssey / Wolf Quest / Ancient Civ.
  • Inspired by DnD, Nobilis, Ctulhulu, Cybperunk 2077 with Factions
  • Merging Interactive Fiction w/Interactive Graphics.
  • Mouse and Keyboard / Touch / Controller style - not point and click.
  • World Lore based on Guardian Lore
  • Progress in a physical board game or campaign can play out on screen* (opt)
  • Shapeshifting Board Game* (opt)
  • Isometric camera view (opt)

The last two concepts are experimental ideas. The concept is a game where people who have a board game or Book who choose to play DnD Campaign style, can also choose to play with a Board Game, Cards, and/or a "Game" where what they do with the board game automatically updates the game, and what they do on the board game automatically updates the board. But how would something like this work without reverting to clunky android/iPad app or controllers? Would wifi or bluetooth be required?


Books

For book readers; the solution is simple. The Book itself will have a:

  • Traditional 'eBook' version with illustrations
  • a Graphic Novel version (the same book but with graphic strips instead - alternatively the 'comic' will be a concept book art type of piece)
  • Interactive Graphic Novel inspired by Memories from "Lost Odyssey" and "Moss"
  • Interactive Encyclopedias

This is one potential concept. Dialogue will be ready in a storybook faction, with voice changing technology to allow 1 or 2 actors to read the script. There will be minimal animation, with words appearing on the screen, and nature elements. Readers can hover over a character name and 'see' what they look like, they can 'hear' the battle playing out. It's an immersive reading experience for people who want to 'see' the world as the author imagined it.

While the concept is 'simple' the implementation is frustrating. There are text-based game engines (and) there are graphic-based game enginges, but there are no game-engines that are meant for inbetween. Since the game leans more heavily on 3D Unity/Unreal/Blender will be the engines of choice. Another challenge is 'style' realism vs. arcane style animation, where 3D mimics 2D. There are more challenges, like will I use an appstore or a webapp? I don't use iphones so what about iphone users. The easiest thing to do at this moment would be to create a browser-based game on Github that can work on mobile and desktop version. Would I use a HTMl5/Javascript library such as Phaser or stick with Unity. Would Unreal work.
