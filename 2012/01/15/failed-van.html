Vandwelling

Jan 15, 2012

In 2010 I joined a vandwelling tribe with my now ex-wife. I met a lot of cool people like Charlene Swankie, who even dropped by to visit me. I was preparing for homelessness and inspired by people who lived either ‘homeless by choice’, or who chose for their ‘home’ to be their life. Getting by on less.

This van was my first vehicle. I thought that once you brought a car, you could live rent free for life. I didn't realize that there was insurance, vehicle maintenance, expensive fuel costs, annual taxes and more. In addition to that I was sold a junk van, it broke down on me before I even got it to the house. Along the line, I started to fear what my rights were in a van. Or what would happen if it got totalled. For me, having my house on wheels was like being a turtle with my house on my back, it gave me confidence and an extended range of movement. The idea that I could lose it all, continues to this day to be a terrifying idea.

Needless to say, the van project didn't last long without income or resources. So we sold it at a loss and I went back to the drawing board. Looking back at it now, if I had, had the emotional and financial resources I have now I would have kept it and converted it.
