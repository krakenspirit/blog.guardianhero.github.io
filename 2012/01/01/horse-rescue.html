Horse Rescues

Jan 1, 2012

My first adventure began at the end of 2011. It was the official year I converted to Buddhism and began identifying my fears and goals, tackling them each one by one. I began volunteering at a Horse Rescue where I stayed until I began WWOOFing and farm sitting. I called my mentor at the time the horse sensei. She eventually established Midnight’s Promise sometime around 2016, it was awesome to watch her do it.

Working with horses was an amazing experience that changed my perspective on the world. I began to believe that I could still achieve my dreams even without the support of other people I had come to depend on. That I could figure out what I want and make it happen, to live and be free. I figured my attachment to money and not having it was my issue, so, I decided I wanted to give up money like Daniel Suelo, live in a cave, and travel by horse and gypsy wagon. After working with horses and realizing how expensive they were I decided to stick to my ‘vandwelling’ plan. It was also while volunteering and sharing my ideas with another fellow volunteer at the time, that I learned about Yurts for the first time.
