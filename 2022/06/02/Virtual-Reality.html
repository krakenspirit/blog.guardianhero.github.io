Virtual Reality Explorations

Jun 2, 2022

In September PSVR went on sale and was available for the first time since COVID’s shortage. The short-term plan was to use PSVR for desktop to play older game titles I loved without VR support with my GOG Library. The long-term plan was to 3D Model in Blender with VR and build game environments with VR in mind.

It was really hard to get the PSVR to work with PC requiring a lot of hacks. Once I got it to work there was more work I needed to do to make it feel seamless. On the playstation VR was fun, but it did not have the effect of making games I usually hate playing “more fun”. And sitting in m y chair with a bulky thing on my head I did not feel immersed. I could only play for 15 minutes at a time before feeling sick, at times my eyes would get dystonic, moving involuntary and I would have to take off the VR. These forced breaks would not have been so bad if the set was not so bulky and heavy.

I am left with one of a few choices:

  1. Purchase Valve Index for Desktop
  2. Stick with PS4 VR - which is locked to Playstatio
  3. Wait for VR to become more lightweight and mainstream

In the meantime, my experiment with VR has been put on hiatus until the technology develops or until the year 2024-2025, after I have built my motion-capture studio for development.
