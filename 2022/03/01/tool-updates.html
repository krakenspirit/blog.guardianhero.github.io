Tool Updates

Mar 1, 2022

My attention has been divided between two aspects of my Guardian Projects. Getting the Monastery up and running at self-sufficient capacity (and) creating art as a byproduct of that monastery.

Medusa Storage and Backup:

  • Expanded storage with Seagate - 18TB and 14TB
  • Repurposed old hard drives from old laptops to expand storage
  • Update: 4-Bay Docking Station to remove hard drives from case for easy access.

Monastery Tools:

  • Soldering Station - for repairs and builds
  • 200w Renogy solar panels w/solar connectors and adapters
  • seeds, wheat berries,
  • Staple guns and accessories,

Monks are the developers of all products we produce, since our work produces little to no income, the ability to feed, clothe and house ourselves while limiting our dependency on money is directly proportionate to skills which overlap with preppers, survivalists and homesteaders.

Mormons are an example of a community where an essential part of their teachings is being prepared. Guardians share a similar philosophy, that we must be prepared to grow most to all of our own food, repair our own electronics, have the capabilities to build our own homes using local resources around us, and more.

Guardians focus on developing crafts related to arts and technology. Different factions and different guardians focus on different elements. Some Guardians work solely with building, others animal husbandry, technology or even a little bit of everything. The Amish are another example of a community of craftsmen known for their woodworking skills.

Guardians work to build facilities that facilitate self-sufficiency. Many monasteries consist of complexes which include a wide range of buildings that also facilitate self-sufficiency and service to the community. Many bigger corporations offer child-care and free food services on site. And ecovillages and other intentional communities have been doing something similar for years now.

My intention is to build something like that for my monastery and spiritual lineage on wheels. Solar panels were an important step. Unfortunately the solar panels are too heavy and bulky for everyday transportation. We will need to utilize the solar panel we have, while looking into flexible more lightweight solar panels, even if they cost more money. Solar and batteries are not the most efficient source of energy storage, but it is easier to transport than geothermal and is the only source of energy I know of that is both portable and does not depend on making fuel runs. At this time we will still need to supplement with fuel for charging but the goal is to use it less. With so much of our infrastructure dependent on technology, it is important that the technology we build consume less energy. We do not have enough energy to run the desktop off-grid for any long period of time. Water collection and storage continues to be a challenge.
