About

This is an image-and-challenge-focused journal. In 2020 I travel around the world acquiring the skills needed to live my life as a Guardian.

This is me testing the page.

The purpose of this blog is to share my individual journey with friends and family. In 2020 I uprooted myself from my martial arts school, which had served as a refuge to me for many years. During my time there I acquired friends who had become family. I wanted to create a way for the people in my life who have become connected to me, to see how and what I'm doing. I don't have Facebook or Instagram and felt that a website and blog would give me more control over my data, privacy, and the freedom to choose my own name. Without requiring permission from the government or Facebook Gods to do so.

Originally I hosted my site on inmotionhosting and then siteground. But decided that it didn't make sense to spend so much money every year on something that wasn't bringing in any money. So, I joined Github and began hosting myself here.

I don't talk about my underlying Guardian Philosophy here. I just take pictures of the adventure I'm on: Crafting Skill Pictures, New Additions to the Family, Constructions, Packed Items, etc. And give a few details of what it is about. Images with minimal text is the primary way that I want to communicate this journey. I don't want to burden people with the details of what amounts to just 'noise'.

As much as I believe in and practice the Guardian philosophy, at the end of the day, my personal truth does not equal ALL TRUTH. And what amounts to a "Becoming a Guardian" journey for me, is really just a personal journey and adventure that I want to share without all the 'noise' attached. When you're here I want you to just see, a friend having an adventure.

For those wondering what the point of my traveling and adventures are, the terminology below will explain it.

Terminology:

All this is just noise, so take it with a grain of salt.

Nation: A Guardian & Hero Network that shares a collective economy. All income a Guardian generates is used to support and serve Guardians & Heroes.

I, Me: Yugen Skye, S.K Murghein, Skye, these are all aliases used to describe the author of this site. Each alias serves a different purpose, and they change. I previously went by Murghein in my personal life, but at present go by Skye. S.K Murghein is used for writing and web development. Yugen Skye is my blog penname.

Spiritual (As I use it): To me, being spiritual means to be in awe of nature, to feel interconnected to everything and every one. When I refer to my spiritual self, I refer to that part of me that I can't touch or see, but can instead develop and explore. My emotional thought patterns, the patterns of me that emerge from the atomic particles that make up all things. My healing, my growth, and my underlying ability to overcome obstacles and preserve in this world, to uplift others and bring a bit of happiness and practical aid to the places I travel. I am heavily philisophical and speak in terms traditionally given a religious context, but am not religious. I believe in everything in relative percents, nothing in absolutes (morals, beliefs, truth, it's all relative to me in this human form). I feel strongly that there is no God in the sense of a 'man or woman in the sky casting judgment' but I do believe in At-One-Ness, and that we are all "Gods" part of this oneness. Even my idea of reincarnation and karma relates back to the whole, collective.

Guardian Monastery: In 2011 I took refuge in the 3 Gems and became a self-ordained buddhist. I called the world my Sangha and began testing and practicing the Buddhist Scriptures. As I questioned my thoughts evolved and eventually the Guardian philosophy was born. A philosophy derived from Buddhism, Daoism and some New Age hippy mumbo jumbo that I cherry picked to create something that worked for me and the personal way that I wanted to live. I identify myself as spiritual agnostic, and serious about it and wanted to dedicate my life to exploring what that meant for me, integrating healing, growth and lifestyle with serving others in some way, shape or form.

I always wanted to live in a Buddhist Monastery. But I also wanted to have a large family, take in children and animals and practice some form of martial arts for the rest of my life. The things I wanted to do, and the emerging belief patterns I developed became incompatible with Buddhism as it is currently practiced today. So. I decided to start training to become a Guardian. Spent 4 years studying Shaolin & Tai chi before switching to Parkour the start of 2020. In 2020 I continue my training as a Guardian, learning to acquire the skills I need to turn the traveling monastery into something stationary with one or more headquarters.

If set up correctly and successfully the Guardian Monastery will be able to exist and support Heroes for multiple generations, with or without any current living Guardian.

Guardian: A Guardian is a person who dedicates their life to healing their own wounds, and then creates a lifestyle/system designed to serve heroes, long after they're gone.

Heroes: Originally Heroes were other nonprofits, later it became a term used for everyday people who decided to take action in their life. At present the word has transitioned to describe children of the monastery. They're called heroes because given the right tools, they have the potential to go out into the world and do great things -- whether or not they choose to become Guardians.

Lighthouse: A lighthouse is the name of a Guardian Monastery. It get's its name from the concept of a lighthouse shining a light out into the sea as a beacon and navigational guide for ships. Each Lighthouse has a Guardian Keeper. The hope is that when a Lighthouse is built, those who resonate with what I am building and doing will come. Even though it's called a Lighthouse, the design of the Guardian Monastery is an eco-castle. A castle built using sustainable and local materials like cob, bamboo, straw. This is why my journey emphasizes learning how to build things using local resources.

Zenratstudios: Originally when I identified as a Buddhist, I created Zenratstudios. It started as a book publishing and game development studio, transitioned at some point to a blog about digital currencies, and now it's a legacy site waiting for me to decide what purpose it will serve. Right now I think the site will be used to host published works, and/or rat genetics. If you can't tell, I Love rats.
