The Guardian Journey

Jun 1, 2015

While volunteering at the horse rescue and natural wildlife museum I experienced a horrible accident that opened up an opportunity for me to go back to school for game development. I did. Not so that I could get into the game business, but so that I could build worlds and games that could help people heal and grow. I saw RPGs and ARGs as a great way to help people, especially people who were socially challenged as I saw myself to be. I planned to take the novels I had written to heal my own wounds, and turn them into socially conscious games people could play, inspired by bioware and interactive fiction. Similar to Flower, Blue Lacuna and Journey.

2015
Zenratstudios

I spent several years studying. And then finally, in November of 2015 after spending a year researching I decided that I had done enough thinking about it. So after planning the concept, I launched Zenratstudios, a book and game publishing company that slowly evolved into Guardian & Hero Nation.


2018
Guardian & Hero Nation


The underlying mechanics of every game is an 'economy', where all elements and aspects of our services were gamified, with the goal of rewarding positive actions. And so it all evolved from games, to hero challenges, to economies, to gardens. You can read a summary of the journey here.

The general idea was to create a pay it forward ecosystem for Guardians & Heroes. Inspired by digital currency and blockchain, I thought I could create a similar system to blockchain that generated Universal Income for those who paid it forward. Hero Points (HP) and Guardian Points (GP) would be the currency. There were a few ways to do that. Heroes would grow their own food and donate any surplus they had, and Guardians would provide the platform. Guardians themselves would give up income to be cared for by the monastery's own economic system so the more they helped Heroes, the more support they would receive -- which in turn would enable them to help more Heroes. The more Heroes helped others, the more aid they would receive. And there was a way for everyone to contribute. Converting waste into compost was one of those ways.

As solid as the idea was, it simply didn't pan out. I didn't have what it takes to launch my own business, but did have what it takes to manage my own family -- so I switched gears and now I just focus on building the Guardian monastery starting with myself and organic waste. The Heroes now refer to children I will take in, and the system for generating security for them as they grow. Guardians are me, and any children or allies who decide to become Guardians as time passes on.

2019
Guardian Monastery

Sometime around 2019, I’m not sure when I gave up on the concept of the Guardian & Hero Nation as something I could get other people to participate in. At this time I decided to take the concepts I had a large scale, and scale it down, not just locally, but on a personal level instead. The idea being, if it’s viable, it will ultimately expand to include more over time. At present the remnants of what I created remains the same.

  • To convert waste into compost i.e. gold
  • To generate income for the Guardian Monastery
  • To create a product for Heroes i.e. children.

My current journey and raison d'être is to gain the skills I need to make my Guardian Monastery a reality. What began as a desire to bring my books to life, a love of worldbuilding became a, why not apply that to the real world. And that's where I'm at now in 2020.
