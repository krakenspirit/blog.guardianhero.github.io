New Plans

Feb 23, 2020

Roadmap Redirects

Took down the old blog and old sites, which were more a ‘personal blog’ chronicling every little detail of the journey. Spent the past several years growing my skills, making mistakes and narrowing down my focus, expertise and niche.

UPDATE (2022): 2020 was a hard year for everyone. I left my Shaolin Kung Fu Family to focus on parkour and to build my monastery full-time. I had originally intended to expand my family and travel and boondock/ wild camp / WWOOF around the country with my Stout Bell Tent and Car, and given time horse and wagon. The starting country was Canada, the end goal was Mongolia. Not only did COVID shut downs end that plan, but I ended up becoming a co-caretaker for my aging parents and grandparents, while also being a homeschooling single parent by choice. Then World War III fell upon the world’s doorstep. Since then I have had to redefine the concept of ‘traveling’ monastery. I have spent 2020-2022 training in horseback riding and acrobatics within my local community. Acrobatics has been incredibly important for both my monastery and my medusa project, as I wanted to be a stunt worker and choregrapher using motion capture for my project, while also merging the spiritual aspects of integrating mind, body and spirit. As of 2022, I continue to train in the endeavor with the focus on achieving backflips.

Progress for backflips has been slowed due to focus on other elements of building the monastery and art. While I still train once a week, it should really be 2-3x/week. My focus is divided between developing other skill sets, which have made me “a jack of all trades, master of none”.

As of this moment I do not have land or amazing martial skills to demonstrate. Pema Chodron once said, you have to “Start Where You Are”. I realized we could still be a monastery even if we did not have land or all the other things I felt we needed to succeed. We could live and practice in the woods, backyards, or a mountain top, so long as we practice. Even if our numbers fell or didn’t grow: The Monastery could live and die with one monk, the last guardian, and still be a monastery, so long as we practiced.
