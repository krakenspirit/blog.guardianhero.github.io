New Plans

Apr 15, 2020

At the start of this year I had planned to camp and travel abroad. To explore Spanish Language Immersion Schools and different schools of martial arts, Krav Maga, Parkour, and even other Shaolin schools in foreign countries. Then Covid-19 happened and the world shut down.

Travelling has been put on hold, but this has not been a bad thing. It has made me realize that I don't need to travel to different schools and countries -- everything I need is right here with me. I am now in the process of doublding down on these four things:

1) My Gardening & Self-Sustainability projects at home.

2) Practicing Guardian-Style MMA

3) Homeschooling, working with and expanding my family full-time.

4) Changing my ‘career path’ and focus.

Guardian Lifestyle:
Garden & Self-sustainability Project

In 2017-2019 I trialed and errored my way through my Gardening & Composting Self-Sustainability project. In 2019 I grew my first plant indoors successfully. And despite a few issues (my indoor light blowing out and deciding not to replace it) this year in 2020 is the first time my garden is bearing fruits and vegetables. In addition 2020 is also the year I've decided to shift my focus to buying land. Instead of saving money to travel and live abroad my primary focus is looking for land, renting out a portion of it for camping and building on the rest.



Guardian Lifestyle: Meditation & Mixed Martial Arts

I've begun establishing a daily Guardian routine & building habits that will lay down the foundation for the rest of my life. The beauty of Parkour is that you don't need to go to any school to learn it. I'm learning how to do kicks, leaps, and flips from my home. While my goal is to practice 3 hours in the morning from 5AM -7AM and then again at night 5PM-7PM. Right now I'm just establishing the habit. I go to bed at 10PM no later than 11 and practice in the morning as soon as I wake up. In the beginning I started by just showing up. I'd walk to the door or the pull up bar and then walk back. Then I started by working out for 5-10 minutes, and then 20, and then 30 and then an hour. My goal is to workout everday 3-6 hours, no less than 1 hour.



Guardian Lifestyle: Technology

I have spent 2011-Present actively acquiring and building upon skills as it relates to technology. 2020 continues this pattern. One of my goals with traveling was to expand my skills as a Web Developer, even though I have always hated Web Development. I realized recently that even though I am terrible at all the things I truly enjoy -- I'd rather spend my time doing those things than Web Developing or even dealing with Cryptocurrency. I am good at those things. But I hate dealing with them. The latter because it demands time and attention that takes away from my family, and the former because I've never enjoyed building with CSS and JS (though SASS and grid frameworks made it slightly more enjoyable -- I still am not a fan).

I'm now working on doing projects that I love. I am combining games, programming, 3D Modeling and world-building like spokes on a wheel around writing. Building a portfolio made up of things that not only I love, but can see myself working on improving for years to come. I want my website and blog to be focused on these projects, because I believe these projects could potentially fund my Guardian Monastery either directly or indirectly by acting as a portfolio for my life's work. If I can find a job utilizing skills I love that help me feed back into my projects that would be amazing -- and if I can build a portfolio showcasing the world I am trying to build for Guardians & Heroes, perhaps people would want to work with me more.



Guardian Lifestyle: Homeschooling & Parenting

This is probably the first time where I've feel I've gotten closer to not only juggling work-life balance but have found a fun way to integrate my hobbies and my projects into my parenting style with room for breaks.



Conclusion:

I find that with scheduling, I still have a lot of time in my day to do these things, but at the expense of having no life outside of these routines (though it is possible). When I'm doing things that I enjoy and love none of this feels like work. I feel so much joy with my lifestyle, and my growing abilities. The biggest help was to create a daily schedule and commit to it just like I would any other 'appointment'. Giving it a time or timeframe plugged into my google Calender.

As a side note. I'm currently trying to figure out what to do with this blog. If it's not an adventure blog, will it focus on being a project blog? Is this blog necessary? I'm not sure. I can't see friends and family coming back just to read this blog as an alternative to me not being on social media. And I can't see strangers being interested in it either. Writing this takes time, and I'm not sure how often I want to consistently update. Once a month certainly. But once a week or day would be too much. It's food for thought.
