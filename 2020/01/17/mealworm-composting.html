Mealworms

Jan 17, 2020

I became interested in mealworms when I learned they can compost styrofoam. I’ve only had them for a few months and already their population has exploded, far faster than the roaches. I hear their waste isn’t that profitable, but who knows. If it isn’t I’ll just experiment and add it to the worm bin, and if it is, then the frass of the mealworms alongside the mealworms would be a good way to generate income for the monastery.

I have three types of mealworms. Buffalo Beetles (Alphitobius diaperinus), Mealworms (Tenebrio Molitor), and Superworms (Zophobos morios). The Superworms are large and so aggressive that they will eat even each other, because of this they will only pupate if no other superworms are present. The mealworms eat styrofoam just like the superworms, but aren't as aggressive eaters as them either. And the Bufflo Beetles are gentler than the mealworms, enough to be placed into the Ivory tank as a cleaner (they won't eat molting Ivory's)

I've overall decided that as useful as the superworms are, they are just too high maintenance. I will not be breeding them and will stick to working with Tenebrio Molitor and Alphitobius Diaperinus until I can come up with an alternative solution for dealing with the superworms (such as separate compartments for each mealworm and then feeding them until they turn into beetles). In the future I hope to be able to work with Dung Beetles to see if they can compost and breakdown human waste. And at a later date Black Soldier Flies. All my composting insects attract fruit flies, and so I'm working on aggressively combatting them.
