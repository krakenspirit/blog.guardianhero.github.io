Bell Tent

Aug 1, 2018

I brought my bell tent a year after building my yurt. Unfortunately, the opportunity to open the bell tent didn’t happen and due to flooding in the basement part of the canvas it was stored in molded. It didn’t effect the interior since the canvas of the tent was rolled inside the groundsheet so that the water never penetrated the canvas.

I first pitched the tent in Spring 2018. I’ve yet to master pitching the bell tent so that it looks like the picture, but Caitlyn from Stout bell tents was really helpful during the first seasonal pitch, and is continuing to help me now in troubleshooting long distance how to resolve the problems I have. The images shown here are the first pitch, flooding, and cleaning issues. In terms of camping, it doesn’t feel practical for short-term camping. So the plan in 2020 is to use the bell tent for long term camping, for stays up to a week, and use the prius for short term camping.

Most of my challenge is folding up the groundtent, esp. if it has rained for the week. Drying and getting the Bell Tent groundsheet clean without a bottom tarp was and continues to be a a challenge. During our first pitch we had to take the tent down and store it dirty due to rain, but brought it outside as soon as it stopped and began working on the ground tarp. There were a lot of grass stains. After getting it as clean as we could get it, we couldn't figure out how to fold it the way they had folded it to get it back in the actual duffel bag. That and it was too heavy to put the top and the bottom together again. So we put the canvas in the duffel bag and stored the groundsheet folded up. This and testing zipper integrity makes for an additional challenge if we're going to camp with the bell tent.
