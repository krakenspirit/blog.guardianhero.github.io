Bell Tent

Aug 1, 2018

I brought my bell tent a year after building my yurt. Unfortunately, the opportunity to open the bell tent didn’t happen and due to flooding in the basement part of the canvas it was stored in molded. It didn’t effect the interior since the canvas of the tent was rolled inside the groundsheet so that the water never penetrated the canvas.

I first pitched the tent in Spring 2018. I’ve yet to master pitching the bell tent so that it looks like the picture, but Caitlyn from Stout bell tents was really helpful during the first seasonal pitch, and is continuing to help me now in troubleshooting long distance how to resolve the problems I have. The images shown here are the first pitch, flooding, and cleaning issues. In terms of camping the plan in 2020 is to use the bell tent for long term camping, for stays up to a week, and use the prius for short term camping, stays lasting less than 24-48 hours.

Most of my challenge is folding up the tent and groundtent, esp. if it has rained for the week. Drying and getting the Bell Tent groundsheet clean without a bottom tarp. What I plan to do is purchase a large 18ft-20ft tarp locally and place it under the bell tent so as to keep it dry and clean. During our first pitch, before I came up with the tarp idea, we had to take the tent down and store it dirty due to rain, but brought it outside as soon as it stopped and began working on cleaning the ground tarp. There were a lot of grass stains. After getting it as clean as we could get it, we couldn't figure out how to fold it the way they had folded it to get it back in the actual duffel bag. That and it was too heavy for me to put the top and the bottom together again. So we put the canvas in the duffel bag and stored the groundsheet folded up. I will spend more time practicing that. When it comes to having to unzip it to decrease the weight when packing, I worry that unzipping the zipper too frequently could decrease the lifespan of the zipper and the tent. So something I will work on doing is actually learning how to carry the full weight of the tent as it is.

March Update: I feel so incredibly grateful and appreciative. After spending time trying to resolve my challenges, I was offered a whole new tent from Caitlyn. I truly believe that my issues with the tent are mainly a user error, my own ignorance. The Bell Tent is going to be my home while I travel throughout the Americas, and for her to do that, even when I insisted she didn't have to, has really warmed my heart and increased my loyalty and respect for this brand. I can't say thank you enough.
