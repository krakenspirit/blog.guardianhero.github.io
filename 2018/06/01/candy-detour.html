Candy Detour

Jun 1, 2018

For several months I tried to make candy for a Guardian Hero fundraiser. The plan was to create little packages of lego candy and lollipops that were healthy and went part of gratitude packages for and from people who were either sick or struggling from mental illness or addictions, the latter being in recovery. It was meant to be healthy and guilt-free. Each note would be handwritten on aged paper, with a waxxed seal. For the kids there would be little positive notes reminding them how worthy and valuable they are in a single phrase, a bit like fortune cookies, but more like, gratitude cookies.

It turns out it is really freaking hard to make candy. I spent 3 months investing the equipment and trying to make healthy candies that looked and tasted good. After a lot of wasted candy, I finally did figure out how to make a good solid gummy. The only problem? It tasted nasty. So we switched to chocolate. It was easier to buy chocolate from sustainable sources and melt it down. The other problem? Aside from having to learn techniques to make good chocolate. Dark chocolate is healthier than white chocolate, and only white chocolate can be dyed.

In the end after all the experimentation I became fascinated by sugar sculpting. It was easy to melt down sugar, and while impossible to make a truly healthy lollipop the beauty of what could be created -- edible glass art was too hard to pass up.

To fund the project I began working at a cafe. I realized the whole point of working at the cafe was to fund the candy, which in turn was meant to fund my Guardian Hero Project...And then I thought, why not just use the income I have for the project itself. So, while working at the cafe I became distracted by the original composting projects and decided to revisit this one later. I actually loved this detour a lot, and still feel there is potential for sugar sculpting to become a hobby of mine in a few years time.
