Mycelium Bricks

Jul 10, 2018

I was particularly interested in mycelium’s ability to break down waste like cardboard and manure, and that some species could even break down plastic. In addition to that I saw incredible potential in using the mycelium as a sustainable building material that could be grown onsite. With the added bonus of being able to supply food like lion’s mane and king oyster once building was complete.

During the Summer I got a job at a cafe and one of the perks was a lot of coffee grounds. I tried my hand at pasteruizing and growing my own mycelium. But there was too much moisture in the bags and it quickly got overtaken by mold. I tried again with manure I had let dry out – but even with using a pressure cooker, nothing grew. I did get a TON of Red Wrigglers that I added to my worm bin, but no mycelium.

My goal is to live a lifestyle that converts all waste into a product that can be used. Mushrooms, Roaches, Worms, Black Soldier Flies, Butterflies, Dung Beetles and other critters that eat waste are a key component to this. Unfortunately the mushroom project was short lived. Financially between investing in the garden both indoors and outdoors, expanding the roaches, the candy and everything else -- dealing with the mycelium became another challenge. I plan to tackle this challenge again, but am not sure if I will do it this year or somewhere down the road.
